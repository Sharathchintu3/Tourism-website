PLACES IN KARNATAKA

1.COORG

Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is a picturesque district in the state of Karnataka, India. Renowned for its lush greenery, coffee plantations, mist-covered hills, and rich cultural heritage, Coorg is often referred to as the "Scotland of India."

2.BADAMI

Badami was the capital of the Chalukya dynasty, which ruled a significant part of southern and central India between the 6th and 8th centuries. The town has historical significance and is known for its contributions to art, architecture, and culture during the Chalukyan period.

3.MYSORE

Mysore, officially known as Mysuru, is a historic city in the southern part of Karnataka, India. Known for its rich cultural heritage, grand palaces, vibrant festivals, and silk and sandalwood industries, Mysore is a popular tourist destination.

4.HAMPI

Hampi, located in the northern part of the Indian state of Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that was once the magnificent capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Hampi is known for its stunning ruins, ancient temples, and unique landscape.

5.GOKARNA

Gokarna is a serene coastal town in the Uttara Kannada district of Karnataka, India. Known for its pristine beaches, ancient temples, and laid-back atmosphere, Gokarna is often referred to as a more peaceful and less commercialized alternative to Goa
