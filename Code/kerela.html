PLACES IN KERELA

1.ARIPPARA WATERFALLS (KOZHIKODE)

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a vibrant city located in the state of Kerala, India. Steeped in history and culture, Kozhikode is known for its significant role in trade, historical landmarks, and coastal beauty. Kozhikode has a rich history and was a major trading hub for spices during the medieval period. It gained prominence as the place where Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama landed in 1498, marking the beginning of European trade with India.

2.ADYANPARA WATERFALLS

Adyanpara Waterfalls is a scenic waterfall located in the Kurumbalangode village near Nilambur in the Malappuram district of Kerala, India. This waterfall is known for its natural beauty and is a popular attraction for nature lovers.

3.MUNNAR

Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, South India. Known for its lush tea plantations, misty hills, and serene landscapes, Munnar is a popular destination for nature lovers, honeymooners, and those seeking a tranquil escape.

5.WAYANAND

Wayanad is a picturesque district in the northeastern part of the Indian state of Kerala. Nestled in the Western Ghats, Wayanad is known for its lush greenery, misty hills, and rich biodiversity. Wayanad is characterized by its rolling hills, dense forests, spice plantations, and numerous waterfalls.
